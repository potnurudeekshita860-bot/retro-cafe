Our Story

Born from a Jazz Flat

Where Records & Roasts First Met

It was 1974. Rajan Pillai, a jazz musician with a coffee obsession, turned his cramped Madras apartment into a listening room that smelled of dark roast and cardamom. Neighbours came for the music, stayed for the coffee — and refused to leave.

Fifty years later, The Vinyl Cup still carries that spirit — analogue warmth in a digital world. We believe great coffee, like great music, demands patience, craft, and soul. Every cup is a small act of resistance against the disposable and the hurried.

We source only from farms we've visited. We roast in-house every morning. We bake from recipes that predate the internet. We are stubbornly, joyfully, unhurriedly old-fashioned.

Farm to Cup

Direct relationships with 12 small farms across Coorg, Wayanad, and the Nilgiris. No middlemen, full traceability.

🔥
Daily Fresh Roasts

Beans roasted every morning by 5am. We never serve anything older than 48 hours. Freshness is non-negotiable.

🎵
Live Analogue Sound

Every weekend, original vinyl on proper turntables. No streaming, no algorithms — just warm, real, imperfect sound.

♻️
Zero Waste Goal

Compostable packaging, spent grounds donated to local gardens, and a 10% discount for customers who bring their own cup.
